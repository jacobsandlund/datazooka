DataZooka is a tool to help find correlations in data. It's designed to make multivariate analysis (more than one variable sampled together) quick and easy.

This demo shows a sample of airplane flights: each flight's distance, arrival delay, boarding time, etc. There are a number of questions that DataZooka can help answer about this dataset.

How does the boarding time relate to the arrival delay?

Looking at only the late flights reveals that they occur later in the day. (Filtering can be done with the filter buttons or by dragging across the chart.)

Of the flights twenty minutes late or later, 78.8% are boarding at 1 PM or later. Flights that are both 20 minutes late or later and leave at 1 PM or later include 13.8% of all flights.

Viewing the comparison chart shows that 63.3% of flights arrive within ten minutes of their designated time. (Dragging a box on the comparison charts shows the percentage in the boxed area.)

Press either of the given buttons, and it will update the chart to look as if there were an equal number of flights at each time of day or arrival delay (similar to conditional probabilities). This demonstrates the large difference between the answers to the questions:

"My plane leaves at this time, how late will it be?"

"My flight arrived this late, when was the boarding time?"

You can also see the degree of correlation by changing the level sliders.
